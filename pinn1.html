Physics-Informed Neural Network

Step-by-step PINN explanation

This page explains how a neural network solves u'(x) + u(x) = 0 with u(0)=1, why physics_residual is an array, and why mean() is used to turn many residuals into one scalar loss.

Why is the mean calculated?

The model checks the differential equation at many collocation points. So the residual is not one number. It is an array of errors:

physics_residual = [r(x₁), r(x₂), ..., r(xₙ)]
Sum of squared residuals

Using sum makes the loss larger when you use more collocation points.

Mean squared residual

Using mean gives the average physics error per point.
